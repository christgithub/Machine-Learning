Unit 2: Exploratory Data Analysis

Although a crucial part of the machine learning process, exploratory data analysis is used across many sectors such as healthcare, retail, finance, marketing, engineering, education, logistics... EDA enables us to extract meaningful insights and make sense of data.

  • Using metrics such as mean, median, standard deviation, quartiles and skewness to acquire a systematic approach to gain a general sense of the data, its size, distribution, feature types and structure.
  • Detecting outliers, missing data and anomalies with format and data types
  • Develop a thorough understanding of various datasets using Python libraries such as Numpy, SciPy, Pandas, Seaborn, Missingno and Matplotlib.
  • Univariate analysis in order to understand and visualise data distribution.
  • Multivariate analysis
  • Generating visual representations and summaries of statistical data such as histograms, scatter plots, density plots or box plots.
